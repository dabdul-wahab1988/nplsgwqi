## 3.7 Deterministic and Bayesian Health-Risk Assessment

Non-carcinogenic health risk was assessed for fluoride and nitrate via ingestion as the sole active exposure pathway, consistent with the absence of dermal and inhalation parameters for these contaminants in the current implementation. The assessment was conducted for three demographic groups: Adults, characterised by a reference body weight of 60 kg and an ingestion rate of 2.5 L/day; Children, characterised by a body weight of 15 kg and an ingestion rate of 1.0 L/day; and Teens, characterised by a body weight of 45 kg and an ingestion rate of 1.5 L/day. These exposure assumptions were applied consistently across both the deterministic and Bayesian components of the risk assessment (see Supplementary Method S2).

Deterministic hazard quotients (HQ) were computed for fluoride ($HQ_{F}$) and nitrate ($HQ_{NO_{3}}$) for each sample and demographic group (Supplementary Method S2.1). Organ-specific health indices were computed by aggregating the relevant HQ values across contaminants:$\ \text{HI}_{\text{skeletal-dental}}$ aggregated fluoride-related dental and skeletal effects, whilst $\text{HI}_{\text{hemato}}$ captured the haematological burden attributable to nitrate. These methods follow established USEPA non-carcinogenic risk assessment conventions <sup>14,15</sup>.

The Bayesian health-risk component (Supplementary Method S2.2) propagated uncertainty in body weight and ingestion rate through hierarchical prior distributions (Supplementary Method S2.3) rather than using fixed point values, thereby generating posterior distributions of HI rather than single-point estimates (Supplementary Method S2.2). Posterior means, 95% credible intervals, and the exceedance probability P(HI \> 1) were reported for each organ-group combination and demographic group, following approaches demonstrated in recent probabilistic groundwater risk studies <sup>16,17</sup>.

**Pathways, thresholds, and uncertainty drivers.** Ingestion-only exposure was selected because dermal and inhalation routes are typically immaterial for fluoride and nitrate under rural borehole use compared to direct consumption, and because pathway-specific parameters needed for defensible dermal modelling were not available in the provided materials. The primary hazard benchmark is HI \> 1 (and constituent-specific HQ \> 1), consistent with USEPA non-carcinogenic risk convention; to improve interpretability for decision-makers, exceedance probabilities were additionally summarised for alternative cutoffs where feasible (Section 4.6). To highlight dominant uncertainty contributors, posterior variance decomposition was performed to partition HI uncertainty between concentration uncertainty and exposure-rate-per-body-weight uncertainty (Table 26), providing a compact analogue to a tornado-style uncertainty ranking under the current model structure.
