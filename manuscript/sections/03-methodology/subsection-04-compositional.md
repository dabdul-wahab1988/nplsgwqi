## 3.4 Compositional Hydrochemical Discovery

Latent hydrochemical processes were identified by applying ILR transformation to the nine-part compositional block, Ca²⁺, Mg²⁺, Na⁺, K⁺, HCO₃⁻, Cl⁻, SO₄²⁻, NO₃⁻, and F⁻, before PCA. The ILR transformation maps the $D$-part simplex into unconstrained $(D - 1)$-dimensional Euclidean space, eliminating the spurious correlations and distance distortions that arise when PCA is applied directly to raw closed compositions <sup>12,13</sup>. Following the sequential binary partition convention, the $k$-th ILR coordinate is:

$$
z_{k} = \sqrt{\frac{k}{k + 1}}\ln\frac{\left( \prod_{j = 1}^{k}x_{j} \right)^{1/k}}{x_{k + 1}},\quad k = 1,\ldots,D - 1
$$

where $x_{1},\ldots,x_{D}$ are the ordered compositional parts ($D = 9$ for the nine-ion block). The number of components to retain was determined objectively by parallel analysis, which compared observed eigenvalues against the 95th-percentile distribution of eigenvalues derived from repeated random permutations of the data matrix. Components were retained only when the observed eigenvalue exceeded the random threshold, providing a data-supported retention criterion rather than an arbitrary scree-plot cutoff (Figure S1).

PCA was performed in ILR space using robust projection logic to guard against the influence of multivariate outliers, which is particularly important at the relatively small sample size of 34. Because ILR loadings are expressed in a coordinate system defined by a chosen orthonormal basis (and thus do not directly map to “raw-ion effects”), loadings were *back-interpreted* by projecting ILR-space components back into CLR space. Concretely, if $V$ denotes the ILR basis matrix (columns spanning the simplex in orthonormal coordinates) and $u$ is a retained ILR-space loading vector for a principal component, then the corresponding CLR loading profile is proportional to $uV^{\top}$ and can be interpreted as a signed log-contrast pattern across ions. Process labels were therefore assigned using the **relative** magnitude and sign structure of these CLR profiles (Table 6; Figure 4), and were refined using independent deterministic geochemical constraints (Section 3.5).

**SBP/basis sensitivity.** To ensure that process labeling was not an artefact of a specific sequential binary partition (SBP), we assessed basis sensitivity by applying two alternative orthonormal rotations of the ILR basis and repeating ILR-PCA. Absolute CLR loading patterns for the retained components were then matched across bases (allowing for component permutation and sign flips) and compared by correlation. The resulting similarity statistics (Table 21) demonstrate that the dominant CLR loading magnitudes are effectively invariant to the tested basis perturbations, supporting the stability of the reported process families.
