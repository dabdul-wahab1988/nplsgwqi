## 3.1 Dataset, Sampling, and Measured Variables

The analytical dataset comprised 34 groundwater samples collected from active public boreholes drilled and maintained by World Vision International across Karaga District, Northern Ghana, in October 2022. The sampling network was distributed across the principal geological units of the district, as shown in Figure 1, with most sites falling within the Bimbila Formation, the dominant host unit, alongside a smaller number of sites on the Bunya and Obosum formations.

Samples were collected in 0.5 L high-density polyethylene bottles that had been thoroughly precleaned with deionized water, 10% nitric acid, and distilled water to eliminate contamination risk. Before each sample was drawn, the borehole was pumped for approximately five minutes to purge the standing column and ensure that representative formation water was collected. Groundwater was then filtered through hand-held syringes fitted with 0.45 μm cellulose acetate filter membranes to remove suspended particulates. The sealed bottles were stored in an ice chest at 4 °C and transported to the Ghana Atomic Energy Commission (GAEC) Laboratory for ion analysis.

Each sample was characterised by 12 measured hydrochemical variables: three field parameters, pH, electrical conductivity (EC), and total dissolved solids (TDS), and nine major dissolved constituents, sodium (Na⁺), potassium (K⁺), magnesium (Mg²⁺), calcium (Ca²⁺), chloride (Cl⁻), sulphate (SO₄²⁻), bicarbonate (HCO₃⁻), nitrate (NO₃⁻), and fluoride (F⁻). Temperature, pH, EC, and TDS were measured in situ using calibrated water quality probes following the field purge. In the laboratory, the anions Cl⁻, SO₄²⁻, F⁻, and NO₃⁻ were determined by ion chromatography using a DIONEX ICS-90 system (Dionex Corporation, California, USA). HCO₃⁻ concentrations were determined by in situ titration using a HACH® digital titrator (model 1690001; Hach Company, Loveland, Colorado, USA). The cations K⁺ and Na⁺ were measured using a flame photometer (FP910-4), while Ca²⁺ and Mg²⁺ concentrations were determined by an AA2OFS Fast Sequential atomic absorption spectrometer. Laboratory QA/QC included routine calibration with standards and verification against certified reference materials appropriate to each analytical method, alongside repeat analyses of standards and samples. The specific CRM catalogue codes and analyte-specific LOD/LOQ metadata were not available within the materials provided for this revision; therefore, all reported concentrations are treated as uncensored, and analytical uncertainty is handled through charge-balance auditing and robustness/sensitivity analyses (Sections 3.2 and 3.9).

**Representativeness and metadata limitations.** The sampling frame reflects WVI-maintained public boreholes and does not include private wells or shallow hand-dug wells; consequently, results should be interpreted as representative of this public-borehole network rather than of all groundwater abstraction points in the district. Well depths, screen intervals, and well-volume or stabilization-log records were not available in the provided dataset, limiting the ability to quantitatively justify the ~5-minute purge duration on a per-well basis. These limitations are explicitly carried into interpretation of seasonality and risk results (Sections 3.1, 3.7, and 6).
