## 3.9 Validation and Supplementary Diagnostics

Several supplementary diagnostic procedures were implemented to assess the robustness of the principal analytical outputs. Bootstrap stability of the absolute CLR loading magnitudes was evaluated by resampling the compositional dataset with replacement across a large number of iterations and recomputing the CLR loading structure for each replicate. For the endpoint-driver models, in-sample R² was reported for the Bayesian horseshoe regression formulations of both NPLS-GWQI and fluoride. The sparse PLS comparator for fluoride was additionally evaluated through nested cross-validation and permutation testing, with the resulting statistics reported in Section 4.5. Sensory-penalty sensitivity for the NPLS-GWQI was examined by comparing score distributions under two model configurations, with and without the TDS-based sensory penalty, and the resulting paired distributions.

Classification robustness for both NPLS-GWQI and N-ISI was additionally evaluated by Bayesian measurement-error propagation under an assumed 5% relative analytical uncertainty; details are presented in supplementary methodology S4. Each observed hydrochemical concentration was treated as a noisy observation of a latent true concentration, posterior samples of the latent concentrations were generated, and each posterior draw was propagated through the full NPLS-GWQI and N-ISI workflows. For each sample, posterior class probabilities were computed as the proportion of posterior draws assigned to each class, and the most probable class and classification confidence were defined as the class with the largest posterior probability and its associated percentage.

Additional robustness checks introduced in response to reviewer comments include (i) PSIS-LOO/WAIC diagnostics and PPC plots for the Bayesian endpoint-driver models (Table 19; Figure S10), (ii) prior-scale sensitivity of driver rankings under alternative global shrinkage scales for the horseshoe (Table 20), (iii) ILR-basis sensitivity of process loading magnitudes under alternative orthonormal bases (Table 21), (iv) benchmarking of NPLS-GWQI against conventional and fuzzy-only WQI baselines including Monte Carlo weight perturbation (Table 22–Table 23; Figure S11), and (v) PHREEQC saturation-index evaluation to support mechanistic fluoride interpretation (Table 18; Figure S9).

To support reproducibility, an analysis workflow diagram and a concise data dictionary describing all input variables are provided in the Supplementary Information materials accompanying this manuscript.
