## 2.1 Location and Settlement Pattern

Karaga District is located in Ghana’s Northern Region, a predominantly rural administrative area situated in the northern part of the country. The district falls within the broader Voltaian sedimentary basin, which underlies much of northern Ghana and extends into adjacent countries <sup>26</sup>. As shown in Figure 1, the district encompasses a network of settlements distributed across a geologically heterogeneous landscape, with the sampling sites concentrated primarily within the dominant Bimbila Formation whilst also capturing communities on the Bunya belt and the southern Obosum terrain.

Settlement in Karaga District is predominantly rural and dispersed, with communities relying heavily on boreholes and shallow wells as principal sources of domestic water supply. Piped water infrastructure is limited, and the availability of treated municipal water is not consistent across the district <sup>1</sup>. This configuration means that the majority of residents draw directly from untreated groundwater sources for drinking, cooking, and household purposes, making the quality of that groundwater a matter of direct public-health concern. Groundwater also supports smallholder livelihoods through dry-season vegetable production, particularly where surface water availability declines sharply outside the wet season <sup>2</sup>. The dual domestic and productive role of groundwater in communities such as those sampled for this study underscores why a multi-dimensional assessment addressing potability, health risk, and irrigation suitability simultaneously is necessary.
