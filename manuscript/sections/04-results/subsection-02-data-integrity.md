## 4.2 Hydrochemical Characterisation

Data integrity was screened using the charge-balance error (CBE) computed from meq/L-converted major ions. Across the 34 samples, CBE ranged from −30.57% to 2.06%, with 64.71% of samples within ±5% and 76.47% within ±10% (Table 17). The distribution (Figure 3) indicates a small subset of comparatively imbalanced samples; given the limited sample size and the absence of additional QA/QC metadata (e.g., analyte-specific LOD/LOQ and field duplicate flags), these values are treated transparently as a limitation rather than as an exclusion trigger, and are carried into the uncertainty-aware diagnostics reported later (Table 19–Table 23).

Descriptive statistics for all 12 measured variables are reported in Table 1. The groundwater is mildly alkaline on average but chemically heterogeneous, with strong upper-tail enrichment in EC, TDS, Na<sup>+</sup>, K<sup>+</sup>, Cl<sup>-</sup>, and F<sup>-</sup> indicating that a minority of highly mineralised samples coexist with a larger body of more moderately chemically evolved waters. Compliance screening against WHO and GSA drinking-water guidelines is summarised in Table 2. HCO₃<sup>-</sup> shows the most frequent exceedance of any measured parameter, whilst Na<sup>+</sup>, K<sup>+</sup> and F<sup>-</sup> each exceed their respective guideline values in 17.65% of samples. EC, TDS, and Cl<sup>-</sup> exceed guidelines less frequently, and Mg<sup>2+</sup> and SO<sub>4</sub><sup>2-</sup> remain fully compliant across the 34 samples. Non-compliance is therefore concentrated in specific dissolved constituents, principally bicarbonate, fluoride, sodium, and potassium, rather than distributed evenly across all parameters. The hydrochemical facies structure, illustrated in Figure 2, is dominated by alkali-bicarbonate waters, particularly the Na-HCO₃ type, with smaller proportions of samples showing K-HCO₃, mixed-bicarbonate, and more chloride-influenced compositions. The normalised parameter spread relative to guideline values, shown in Figure S2, confirms that bicarbonate enrichment and episodic fluoride and sodium excursions constitute the principal departure from potability standards.
