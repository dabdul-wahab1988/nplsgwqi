## 4.6 Deterministic and Bayesian Non-Carcinogenic Health Risk

Deterministic health-risk results are reported in Table 10 and Figure 8. The risk profile is clearly age-ordered for fluoride: children recorded the highest mean $HQ_{F}$ at 1.493, followed by adults at 0.933 and teens at 0.746. Corresponding mean $HQ_{NO_{3}}$ values were substantially lower across all demographic groups, at 0.327 for children, 0.204 for adults, and 0.163 for teens, and nitrate remained predominantly sub-threshold across the sampled waters. Fluoride distributions retained upper tails above the hazard quotient threshold of 1, especially in children, whilst nitrate did not produce comparable upper-tail exceedances in any demographic group. The deterministic skeletal-dental health index ($HI_{skeletal\ dental}$) was elevated in children relative to other groups, reflecting the combined effect of lower body weight and higher relative ingestion rate in that demographic. The principal deterministic burden is therefore fluoride-driven skeletal-dental risk, with children constituting the most vulnerable receptor group.

Bayesian posterior health-risk results are presented in Table 11 and Figure 9. The Bayesian results preserve the deterministic ranking whilst making exposure uncertainty explicit. Children recorded the highest posterior mean skeletal-dental health index at HI = 2.155 with P(HI \> 1) = 0.6875, followed by adults at a posterior mean of 1.315 and P(HI \> 1) = 0.4625, and teens at a posterior mean of 1.200 and P(HI \> 1) = 0.4000. Hematological posterior means remained below unity for all groups at 0.556 for children, 0.364 for adults, and 0.316 for teens, confirming that the nitrate-related haematological burden was sub-threshold under uncertainty-aware estimation. Credible intervals are wide, reflecting the propagated exposure uncertainty, but the vulnerability ordering across demographic groups is stable across the posterior distribution, supporting the conclusion that elevated fluoride-related skeletal-dental risk in children is robust to parameter uncertainty.

To support multi-threshold decision use, exceedance probabilities were additionally summarised for alternative cutoffs (Table 27). For example, children have P(HI\_SD \> 2) = 0.3625 under the modelled dry-season exposure scenario, whereas nitrate-related P(HI\_hemato \> 1) remains low across groups. Uncertainty decomposition indicates that skeletal-dental HI uncertainty is driven primarily by uncertainty in fluoride concentration (Table 26), with ingestion-rate-per-body-weight uncertainty contributing the remainder under the ingestion-only model structure.
