## 1.4 Objectives

To operationalize this framework, the investigation is structured around five specific objectives: (i) to assess groundwater quality using a neutrosophic PLS-based groundwater quality index (NPLS-GWQI) with bootstrapped dynamic variable-importance-in-projection weighting and aesthetic–sensory sensitivity handling, (ii) to identify latent hydrochemical signatures using ILR-PCA with parallel analysis and to interpret them against deterministic geochemical constraints, (iii) to profile the relative hydrochemical drivers of NPLS-GWQI and fluoride using Bayesian horseshoe regression on an augmented predictor block. The horseshoe prior is specifically adopted here to aggressively shrink background noise and handle high dimensionality, ensuring that driver profiling remains mathematically robust and testable despite the restricted sample size, with supplementary sparse-PLS comparator diagnostics applied for validation, (iv) to estimate deterministic and Bayesian non-carcinogenic health risk for fluoride and nitrate across three demographic groups: Adults, Children, and Teens, with uncertainty characterised through posterior distributions and exceedance probabilities, and (v) to evaluate irrigation suitability through entropy-weighted neutrosophic irrigation suitability indexing (N-ISI) derived from seven standard irrigation quality parameters.

Ultimately, this study offers an integrated and transferable framework for analogous Voltaian sedimentary groundwater settings across the region.
