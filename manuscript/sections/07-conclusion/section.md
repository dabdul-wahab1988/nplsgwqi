# 6 Conclusion

This study applied a dual-space compositional process framework to assess groundwater quality, latent hydrochemical processes, Bayesian driver profiles, health risk, and irrigation suitability in Karaga District, northern Ghana, using a dataset of 34 groundwater samples characterised by 12 measured hydrochemical variables. The findings address each of the six study objectives and advance the methodological treatment of compositional hydrochemical data in the assessment of groundwater systems within the Voltaian sedimentary basin.

Addressing the first objective, ILR-based facies characterisation established that groundwater in Karaga District is predominantly Na-HCO₃ in type, hosted primarily within the Bimbila Formation, with rock-weathering control dominating 30 of 34 samples in the Gibbs diagnostic framework. On the second objective, NPLS-GWQI classification placed 25 of 34 samples in the excellent category, with only 8 samples in degraded classes and 4 classified as unsuitable, and bootstrapped N-VIP weighting confirmed that EC and salinity-related variables dominate the water-quality signal.

The third objective yielded five latent hydrochemical processes through ILR-PCA with parallel analysis, Na-Cl enrichment, NO₃-Ca association, Mg-F association, K-F association, and Na-HCO₃ exchange, validated by deterministic geochemical constraints including Gibbs-domain assignment, chloro-alkaline exchange indices, and elevated Na/Cl ratios. On the fourth objective, Bayesian horseshoe regression identified EC as the dominant positive driver of NPLS-GWQI at 43.18%, with Na acting as the principal suppressing term at 14.86%, and revealed that the fluoride endpoint is governed by competing $CAI_{2}$ suppression and EC promotion at approximately equal magnitudes, confirming the multicausal character of fluoride enrichment in this setting.

Addressing the fifth objective, children emerged as the most vulnerable demographic in both deterministic and Bayesian health-risk assessments. Deterministic mean $HQ_{F}$ for children was 1.493, and Bayesian analysis returned a posterior mean skeletal-dental HI of 2.155 with P(HI \> 1) = 0.6875, whilst nitrate-related hematological burden remained clearly sub-threshold across all demographic groups. On the sixth objective, irrigation suitability was predominantly marginal, with 23 of 34 samples in the marginal N-ISI class, PI, PS, and KR identified as the highest-weight entropy indices, and only 3 samples classified as unsuitable.

The study contributes a transferable, uncertainty-aware analytical architecture to the groundwater assessment literature for northern Ghanaian and analogous Voltaian sedimentary settings. Key limitations include the single-season sampling design, the relatively small sample size of 34, and the absence of trace-metal measurements, which together constrain temporal generalisation and health-risk scope. Future work should incorporate wet-season and dry-season paired sampling campaigns, expand the chemical panel to include trace metals, and extend the spatial coverage of the sampling network to capture the full lithostratigraphic variability of the district more completely.
