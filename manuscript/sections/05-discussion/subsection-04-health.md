## 5.4 Public-Health Interpretation of Fluoride and Nitrate

The finding that children exhibit the highest deterministic fluoride hazard quotient (mean $HQ_{F}$ = 1.493; Table 10; Figure 8), with the mean value exceeding the unit threshold whilst adult and teen values remain sub-threshold on average, is consistent with the established pattern in northern Ghana groundwater studies. Children have consistently been identified as the most vulnerable receptor group in probabilistic fluoride risk assessments across northern Ghana, with studies in the Upper East Region <sup>8</sup> and Bongo District <sup>7</sup> both reporting non-carcinogenic risk in children at boreholes where adult exposure levels remained sub-threshold. The present findings confirm that this vulnerability gradient applies in Karaga District groundwater and is attributable to the combined effect of lower body weight and higher relative ingestion rate in children, which produce greater unit-mass fluoride exposure per unit concentration than in adults or teens.

The Bayesian results (Table 11; Figure 9) preserve this vulnerability ordering whilst revealing the degree of uncertainty surrounding the point estimates. The posterior mean skeletal-dental HI for children of 2.155 with P(HI \> 1) = 0.6875 indicates that there is approximately a 69% probability that the true population-level skeletal-dental health index for children exceeds the threshold of concern under the modelled exposure scenario. For adults, P(HI \> 1) = 0.4625 and for teens it is 0.4000, reflecting meaningfully lower but still non-negligible probabilities of exceedance. These Bayesian exceedance probabilities communicate risk more completely than the deterministic point estimates alone, in line with arguments made by Kaur et al.<sup>14</sup> and Ma et al.<sup>16</sup> for uncertainty-propagated health-risk characterisation. The contrast between the fluoride-driven skeletal-dental burden and the clearly sub-threshold haematological indices for nitrate across all groups (posterior means of 0.556, 0.364, and 0.316 for children, adults, and teens respectively) underlines that fluoride is the principal chemical health stressor in this groundwater system, with nitrate posing a lower but non-negligible concern that warrants continued monitoring particularly where sanitary pressure is suspected to elevate concentrations.
